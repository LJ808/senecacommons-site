Wage-Productivity Decoupling — EPI STATIC

1948–1979: productivity grew 2.5%/yr, compensation 2.1%/yr — nearly locked together. 1979–2025: productivity grew 1.4%/yr, compensation only 0.6%/yr. The gap is the mechanism, not a side effect.

Source: Economic Policy Institute, epi.org/productivity-pay-gap. EPI analysis of unpublished BLS Total Economy Productivity data, BLS Current Employment Statistics, BLS Employment Cost Trends, BLS Consumer Price Index, and BEA National Income and Product Accounts. Static image, not a live feed — EPI updates this chart periodically, not via API.
