How Bonus TDS Gets Calculated in India

Performance bonuses in India attract TDS at the marginal income tax rate applicable to the employee's total annual income including the bonus. Employers calculate TDS using one of two methods: the marginal rate method (apply the slab rate applicable to total income directly to the bonus), or the annualised method (add the bonus to the annualised salary, compute full-year tax, subtract tax already deducted on regular salary, deduct the difference from the bonus).

The annualised method produces a more accurate TDS figure when bonuses push income across a slab boundary. Under the New Regime for FY 2025–26, the 20% slab begins at ₹12 lakh and the 30% slab at ₹15 lakh. An employee earning ₹11.5 lakh in regular salary who receives a ₹1 lakh bonus crosses the 20% threshold — correct TDS calculation must account for the partial slab crossover, not simply apply 20% to the whole bonus.

Statutory bonus under the Payment of Bonus Act applies to employees whose basic salary plus DA does not exceed ₹21,000/month. The Act mandates a minimum of 8.33% and permits a maximum of 20% of basic salary (or ₹7,000/month, whichever is higher) as the computation base. Statutory bonus also attracts income tax — it forms part of gross salary and gets taxed at the applicable slab rate.

Does my employer deduct the correct TDS on my bonus?
Many employers deduct TDS on bonus at a flat 10% or at the basic slab rate without accounting for the total annual income including the bonus. This often results in under-deduction — the employee then owes additional tax at year-end filing. Use this calculator's annualised method to verify whether your employer's TDS figure accurately reflects your actual tax liability on the bonus amount.
Who qualifies for statutory bonus under the Payment of Bonus Act?
Employees working in establishments with 20 or more workers, whose basic salary plus DA does not exceed ₹21,000 per month, qualify for statutory bonus. The bonus computation base caps at ₹7,000/month or the applicable minimum wage, whichever is higher. Minimum payout: 8.33% of that base. Maximum: 20%. Employers must pay within 8 months of closing the financial year.
Should I invest my bonus or prepay a home loan?
The decision reduces to comparing guaranteed post-tax return from home loan prepayment versus expected post-tax investment return. Home loan interest at 8.5% saves 8.5% guaranteed (plus Old Regime Section 24 deduction benefit if applicable). A diversified equity SIP targets 10–12% CAGR but carries market risk. For loans above 8.5% and investment horizons below 5 years, prepayment typically wins. For longer horizons with equity exposure, investment often outperforms.
