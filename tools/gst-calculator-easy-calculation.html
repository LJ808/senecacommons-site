What does "add GST" vs "remove GST" mean?
Add GST means your number is the price before tax, and you want to know the final price a customer pays — GST gets added on top. Remove GST means your number is the final price already including tax, and you want to know what the original price was before tax — GST gets pulled back out. Always check which one matches the number you're starting with.
Which GST rate should I use?
18% applies to most goods and services in India. 5% commonly applies to packaged food and footwear under ₹1,000. 12% covers processed food and some services. 28% applies to luxury items and tobacco. 0% applies to fresh produce, milk, and books. If you have an invoice, the rate is usually printed on it directly.
